🎙 Constitutional AI Narration

Fundamental Rights (Articles 12–35)

The Constitution of India guarantees six major Fundamental Rights to every citizen. These rights protect individual freedom and ensure equality before law. They include Right to Equality, Freedom, Protection from Exploitation, Freedom of Religion, Cultural & Educational Rights, and Constitutional Remedies.

Fundamental Duties (Article 51A)

Fundamental Duties remind every citizen that rights come with responsibilities. They include respecting the Constitution, national flag and anthem, promoting harmony, protecting environment, safeguarding public property, and striving towards excellence in all spheres.

Anti-Corruption Laws in India

India has strong legal mechanisms to fight corruption including Prevention of Corruption Act 1988, Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act 2013, Right to Information Act 2005, and Whistle Blowers Protection Act 2014.
