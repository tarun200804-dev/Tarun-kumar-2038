Fundamental Rights

Article 14 – Right to Equality

Article 14 guarantees equality before law and equal protection of laws. It ensures that no citizen is above the law and no citizen is denied protection. The rule of law forms the backbone of democracy. The State cannot act arbitrarily. Reasonable classification is allowed but discrimination is prohibited. The judiciary ensures fairness through judicial review. Equality strengthens democracy, builds trust in institutions, and protects citizens from misuse of power. Understanding Article 14 empowers citizens to challenge injustice and defend their dignity under the Constitution of India.

Article 19 – Right to Freedom

Article 19 grants six freedoms including speech, expression, movement, assembly, association and profession. These freedoms enable citizens to actively participate in democracy. Freedom of speech allows criticism of government policies. Freedom of movement strengthens national unity. Freedom of profession ensures economic liberty. Reasonable restrictions exist to protect public order and sovereignty. Article 19 is essential for democratic governance and responsible citizenship.

Article 21 – Right to Life

Article 21 ensures no person shall be deprived of life or liberty except by procedure established by law. The Supreme Court expanded it to include dignity, privacy, clean environment and legal aid. It protects citizens from arbitrary state action. Right to life means right to live with dignity and respect. It is one of the most powerful articles of the Constitution.

Fundamental Duties

Article 51A lists duties of every citizen including respecting the Constitution, protecting environment, promoting harmony and safeguarding public property.
