Fundamental Rights

All citizens are equal before the law and prohibited from discrimination based on religion, caste, sex, or place of birth. Abolition of untouchability and titles are ensured.

Includes freedom of speech, assembly, association, movement, and right to practice any profession. Protection in case of arrest or preventive detention.

Prohibits human trafficking, forced labor, and child labor under 14 years.

Ensures religious freedom and prevents religious instruction in state-funded institutions.

Preserves culture, language, and education rights of minorities.

Allows citizens to move the Supreme Court or High Courts for enforcement of fundamental rights.

Fundamental Duties

Abide by the Constitution and respect its ideals and institutions.

Promote harmony and spirit of common brotherhood amongst all people of India.

Preserve the rich heritage of India’s composite culture.

Protect natural environment, forests, lakes, rivers, and wildlife.

Anti-Corruption Laws

Defines offenses of bribery and corruption, and prescribes punishments.

Establishes institutions to investigate corruption cases against public officials.

Protects individuals exposing corruption in government institutions.
