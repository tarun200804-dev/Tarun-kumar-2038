Fundamental Rights

Article 14 – Right to Equality

Article 14 guarantees equality before law and equal protection of laws. It ensures no citizen is above the law and no citizen is denied protection. This prevents arbitrary state action and strengthens rule of law. Equality is the foundation of democracy and ensures justice, fairness and non-discrimination in governance.

Article 19 – Right to Freedom

Article 19 grants freedom of speech, expression, movement, assembly and profession. These freedoms empower citizens to question authority and participate in democracy. Reasonable restrictions protect sovereignty and public order.

Article 21 – Right to Life and Liberty

Article 21 protects life and personal liberty. It includes dignity, privacy, clean environment and legal protection. It is one of the most powerful safeguards in the Constitution.

Fundamental Duties

Every citizen must respect the Constitution, protect unity, promote harmony, safeguard public property and protect the environment. Duties balance rights and create responsible citizenship.

Anti-Corruption Laws

Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988

This Act criminalizes bribery and misuse of public office. It ensures accountability of public servants and promotes integrity in governance.

Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 2013

This Act establishes independent authorities to investigate corruption against public officials and ministers.

Right to Information Act, 2005

RTI empowers citizens to access government information and enhances transparency in governance.
