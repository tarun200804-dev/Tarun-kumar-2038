Fundamental Rights

Article 14 – Right to Equality

Article 14 guarantees equality before the law and equal protection of the laws within the territory of India. It ensures that no citizen is treated unfairly on arbitrary grounds. The principle of rule of law ensures that governance remains transparent and accountable. This Article prevents discrimination and strengthens democratic justice.

Article 19 – Right to Freedom

Article 19 grants freedom of speech, movement, assembly, association and profession. These freedoms empower citizens to question, participate and contribute to democracy. Reasonable restrictions protect sovereignty and public order.

Fundamental Duties

Fundamental Duties under Article 51A remind citizens that rights must be balanced with responsibilities. Citizens must respect the Constitution, protect national unity, preserve cultural heritage, protect environment and promote harmony. Duties strengthen democratic discipline and moral accountability.

Anti-Corruption Laws

Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988

The Prevention of Corruption Act criminalizes bribery and abuse of official power. Public servants accepting illegal gratification face prosecution. The Act was strengthened in 2018 to enhance accountability and transparency in governance.

Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 2013

This Act established independent anti-corruption authorities to investigate complaints against ministers and public officials. It strengthens institutional accountability and protects democratic integrity.

Right to Information Act, 2005

The RTI Act empowers citizens to request information from public authorities. It enhances transparency and reduces corruption by enabling public oversight.
