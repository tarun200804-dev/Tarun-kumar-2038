Fundamental Rights (Articles 12–35)

Right to Equality (Articles 14-18)

Ensures equality before law and prohibits discrimination on religion, race, caste, sex or place of birth.

Right to Freedom (Articles 19-22)

Includes freedom of speech, expression, movement, association, residence and profession.

Right Against Exploitation (Articles 23-24)

Prohibits human trafficking, forced labour and child labour.

Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25-28)

Ensures religious freedom and secularism.

Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29-30)

Protects rights of minorities to conserve culture and establish institutions.

Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

Citizens can approach Supreme Court if rights are violated.

Fundamental Duties (Article 51A)

Every citizen must respect the Constitution, National Flag, National Anthem, protect environment, promote harmony and safeguard public property.

Anti-Corruption Laws

Prevention of Corruption Act 1988 criminalizes bribery and corruption. Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act 2013 ensures investigation of corruption cases. Right to Information Act 2005 empowers citizens to access government records.
